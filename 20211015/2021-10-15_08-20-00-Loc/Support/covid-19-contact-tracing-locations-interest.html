COVID-19: Contact tracing locations of interest

Locations of interest for people who may be contacts of COVID-19 cases in the community. Contacts who attended one of the locations in New Zealand during the relevant times are asked to follow the directions for that location.

This page is updated regularly throughout the day with any new or updated locations of interest.

What to do if you have been at a location listed below

Please see the locations of interest in the table and on the map below. If you visited a location of interest during the relevant time, follow the health instructions provided. 

If required, call Healthline on 0800 358 5453 or you can record your visit online. If you record your visit online, you do not need to call Healthline – our contact tracers will call or email you.

If you are required to isolate you must do so until you have spoken to Healthline. They will advise you and your household members on what you need to do.

However, if you are a casual contact who has been at a location of interest, you do not need to record your visit online or by phoning Healthline.

Please note that there are section 70 public health orders in place that require you to follow health orders. This also applies to household contacts for some locations (unless the contact is a vaccinated health worker or managed isolation and quarantine worker, and they meet certain criteria). 

Anyone who returns a positive COVID-19 test is required to enter a managed isolation facility for 14 days.

If you have been contacted by a district health board, the Ministry of Health or a district health board public health unit, please follow their advice.

Bay of Plenty locations of interest removed

Locations of interest for the Bay of Plenty have been removed. The person who visited these locations has returned a negative test and was therefore not infectious.

If you used our form to record a visit you will be called and advised you are not a contact.

Northland locations of interest

If you were in Whangarei, Paihia, Onerahi or Kawakawa between Saturday, 2 October and Wednesday 6 October, you may have been at a location of interest. More specific details will be updated on this page as information comes to hand but it is recommended that you go and get tested, even if you don’t have COVID-19 symptoms. Testing stations are open throughout the weekend.
